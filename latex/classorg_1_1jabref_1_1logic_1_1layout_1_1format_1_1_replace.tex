\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Formatter that does regexp replacement.

To use this formatter, a two-\/part argument must be given. The parts are separated by a comma. To indicate the comma character, use an escape sequence\+: \textbackslash{}, For inserting newlines and tabs in arguments, use ~\newline
 and \textbackslash{}t, respectively.

The first part is the regular expression to search for. Remember that any commma character must be preceded by a backslash, and consequently a literal backslash must be written as a pair of backslashes. A description of Java regular expressions can be found at\+: \href{http://java.sun.com/j2se/1.4.2/docs/api/java/util/regex/Pattern.html}{\texttt{ http\+://java.\+sun.\+com/j2se/1.\+4.\+2/docs/api/java/util/regex/\+Pattern.\+html}}

The second part is the text to replace all matches with.

For instance\+: \textbackslash{}format\mbox{[}Replace(and,\&)\mbox{]}\{\begin{DoxyAuthor}{Author}
\} \+: will output the \char`\"{}author\char`\"{} field after replacing all occurences of \char`\"{}and\char`\"{} by \char`\"{}\&\char`\"{}
\end{DoxyAuthor}
